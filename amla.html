Interactive Learning of the Amla

Botanical Name:

Amla (Phyllanthus emblica)
Kingdom: Plantae
Order: Malpighiales
Family: Phyllanthaceae
Genus: Phyllanthus
Species: emblica

Common Names:

Amla is known by various names across different regions:
Hindi: आमला (Amla)
Gujarati: આમલક (Amalak)
Sanskrit: आमलकी (Āmalakī)
English: Indian Gooseberry, Emblic Myrobalan, Malacca Tree

Uses in Ayush:


Amla is renowned for its extensive health benefits, including:
Rich in Nutrients:
Amla is exceptionally high in Vitamin C, antioxidants, and iron, which contribute to its health-promoting properties.
Medicinal Properties:
It is used to treat various ailments such as:
Common cold and fever
Digestive issues (as a laxative and digestive aid)
Liver tonic and blood purifier
Respiratory disorders (reducing cough and throat infections)
Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects
Enhancing immunity and reducing stress
Cosmetic Uses:
Amla is commonly found in shampoos and hair oils due to its ability to strengthen hair, reduce hair fall, and combat dandruff.

Directions to Use:


How to Use Amla for Instant Relief
Methods of Consumption
Raw Fruit:
Eating fresh amla fruit can provide immediate health benefits.
Amla Juice:
Diluting amla juice with water or honey can enhance its palatability and effectiveness.
Powder Form:
Amla powder can be mixed with water or yogurt for consumption.
Infused in Honey:
Mixing amla with honey can create a potent remedy for sore throats and coughs.

Dosage:

Fresh Amla:
1-2 fruits daily.
Amla Juice:
15-30 ml (1-2 tablespoons) mixed with water, taken once or twice a day.
Amla Powder:
1-2 teaspoons (3-6 grams) mixed with water or yogurt, taken once daily.
