Interactive Learning of the Brahmi

Botanical Name:

Bacopa monnieri

Common Names:

English: Indian pennywort, water hyssop
Hindi: Barmi, Jalanima
Sanskrit: Brahmi

Uses in Ayush:

It helps the body resist stress and promoting overall wellness. Brahmi is traditionally used for various therapeutic purposes, including:
• Cognitive Enhancement:
Improves memory, learning, and concentration.
• Anxiety and Stress Relief:
Acts as an anxiolytic, helping to reduce anxiety levels.
• Neurological Disorders:
Used in the management of conditions such as epilepsy and schizophrenia.
• Anti-inflammatory Properties:
Helps in treating inflammatory conditions and skin diseases.
• Antioxidant Effects:
Protects against oxidative stress.

Directions to Use:

For quick relief, Brahmi can be consumed in the following forms:
1. Fresh Juice:
• Take 10-20 ml of fresh Brahmi juice mixed with honey to enhance taste and efficacy. This can help in calming the mind and improving cognitive functions.
2. Powdered Form:
• Mix 1-2 grams of Brahmi powder with water or milk. This can be taken once or twice daily.
3. Capsules:
• Brahmi is available in capsule form, typically containing 300-500 mg of extract. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for dosage.
4. Tea:
• Brew Brahmi leaves in hot water to make a herbal tea, which can be consumed once or twice a day.

Dosage:

The recommended dosage of Brahmi varies based on the form used:
• Fresh Juice:
10-20 ml daily.
• Powder:
1-2 grams, once or twice daily.
• Capsules:
300-500 mg, as per product guidelines.
