Interactive Learning of the tulasi

Botanical Name:


Ocimum sanctum (Linn) or Ocimum tenuiflorum

Common Names:

Tulsi, Tulasi, Holy Basil

Uses in Ayush:


Tulsi is known for its extensive medicinal properties, including:
• Respiratory Health: Used to alleviate cough, bronchitis, and asthma.
• Digestive Aid: Helps with indigestion, diarrhea, and gas.
• Stress Relief: Acts as an adaptogen to help the body cope with stress.
• Anti-inflammatory: Reduces inflammation and pain, beneficial for arthritis.
• Antimicrobial: Exhibits broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity.
• Fever and Cold: Commonly used in treating fevers and flu-like symptoms.
• Skin Health: Used for skin diseases and insect bites.

Directions to Use:


Herbal Tea
1. Ingredients:
Fresh or dried tulsi leaves (about 5-10 leaves or 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves).
2. Preparation:
• Boil water (about 1 cup).
• Add the leaves to the boiling water.
• Let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
• Strain and drink. You may add honey for taste.
Tulsi Juice
1. Ingredients:
Fresh tulsi leaves (about 10-15 leaves).
2. Preparation:
• Wash and crush the leaves to extract juice.
• Mix the juice with a glass of water and drink it fresh.
Tulsi Powder
1. Ingredients:
Dried tulsi leaves (1 teaspoon).
2. Usage:
• Mix the powder with honey or warm water and consume it for quick relief from cough and cold

Dosage:

Herbal Tea: 1-2 cups daily.
• Juice: 1-2 tablespoons mixed with water, taken once or twice daily.
• Powder: 1 teaspoon daily, can be mixed with food or water.
There is no standardized dosage, but some general guidelines:
Leaf juice:
1-4 grams per dose, 2-3 times daily
Decoction of root and bark:
30 grams per dose, 2-3 times daily for 3 days
Dried leaves for smoking:
As needed for relief
Always consult a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner for personalized dosage recommendations based on your specific condition and body constitution.
