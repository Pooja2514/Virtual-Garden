Interactive Learning of the Peppermint

Botanical Name:

Mentha × piperita

Common Names:

Peppermint
Mint
Other Names:
Bohe (in Chinese medicine)

Uses in Ayush:


Peppermint is recognized for its numerous medicinal properties, including:
• Digestive Aid:
Helps relieve indigestion and bloating.
• Nausea Relief:
Effective for reducing nausea and vomiting, particularly in motion sickness.
• Headache Relief:
The menthol in peppermint can help alleviate tension headaches.
• Respiratory Health:
Acts as a decongestant and can soothe sore throats.
• Pain Relief:
Topical application of peppermint oil can relieve muscle and joint pain due to its cooling effect.
• Flavoring Agent:
Widely used in food, beverages, and dental products for its flavor and aroma

Directions to Use:


For quick relief
1. Peppermint Tea:
• Preparation:
Steep fresh or dried peppermint leaves in hot water for 5-10 minutes.
• Usage:
Drink to relieve digestive issues or nausea.
2. Peppermint Oil:
• Topical Application:
Dilute peppermint oil with a carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil) and apply to the temples for headache relief or to sore muscles for pain relief.
• Inhalation:
Add a few drops of peppermint oil to a bowl of hot water and inhale the steam to relieve nasal congestion.
3. Peppermint Capsules:
• Dosage:
Typically, enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules are taken at a dosage of 0.2 to 0.4 mL, 2 to 3 times daily, for digestive relief.

Dosage:
