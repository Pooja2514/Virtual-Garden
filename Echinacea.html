Botanical Name:

Echinacea purpurea

Common Names:

Eastern Purple Coneflower
Purple Coneflower
Hedgehog Coneflower
Coneflower
Native Range: Eastern and central North America,
particularly in dry open woods,
prairies,
and meadows.

Uses in Ayush:


Echinacea is primarily used for
• Immune Support:
Often taken to prevent or treat colds and respiratory infections.
• Anti-inflammatory Effects:
May help reduce inflammation and pain.
• Wound Healing:
Traditionally used for skin ailments and to promote healing.
• Antimicrobial Properties:
Some studies suggest it may help fight infections.

Directions to Use:


For quick relief,Echinacea can be used in various forms, including:
• Tea:
Steep 2-3 grams of dried Echinacea root or leaves in hot water for 10-15 minutes.
Drink 2-3 times daily.
• Tincture:
Take 20-30 drops of Echinacea tincture (liquid extract) in water, several times a day, especially at the onset of cold symptoms.
• Capsules:
Follow the dosage instructions on the product label, typically ranging from 300-500 mg, taken 2-3 times daily.

Dosage:


• Dried Root or Leaf:
2-3 grams per day, divided into multiple doses.
• Tincture:
20-30 drops, 3 times daily.
• Capsules:
300-500 mg, taken 2-3 times daily.
Important Considerations
• Duration of Use:
It is generally recommended to use Echinacea for no longer than 8 weeks at a time.
