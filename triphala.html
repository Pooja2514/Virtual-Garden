Interactive Learning of the Triphala

Botanical Name:

Triphala consists of the following three plants:
• Emblica officinalis (commonly known as Amla or Indian Gooseberry)
• Terminalia chebula (commonly known as Haritaki)
• Terminalia bellirica (commonly known as Bibhitaki)

Common Names:

• Amla: Amla or Indian Gooseberry
• Haritaki: Harad
• Bibhitaki: Baheda or Vibhidaka

Uses in Ayush:

Triphala is revered for its numerous health benefits, including:
• Digestive Health:
Acts as a natural laxative, alleviating constipation and promoting regular bowel movements.
• Antioxidant Properties:
Rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, it helps combat oxidative stress and inflammation.
• Weight Management:
Aids in detoxification and enhances nutrient absorption, which may assist in weight loss.
• Dental Health:
Used in mouthwashes to reduce plaque and gingivitis.
• Skin and Hair Health:
Promotes healthy skin and hair by delaying aging signs and preventing hair fall.
• Overall Wellness:
Known as a rejuvenator (Rasayana), it supports the immune system and enhances overall vitality.

Directions to Use:


How to Use for Instant Relief:
For immediate relief, particularly for digestive issues, Triphala can be used in the following ways:
1. Powder Form:
Mix 1 teaspoon (approximately 5 grams) of Triphala powder in a glass of warm water. This can be taken before meals to aid digestion and relieve constipation.
2. With Honey:
Combine Triphala powder with a teaspoon of honey to form a paste. This can be consumed directly for quick relief from digestive discomfort.
3. With Ghee:
Mix Triphala powder with a small amount of ghee and warm water. This soothing drink can help ease digestive issues.

Dosage:

The typical dosage of Triphala varies based on individual needs and health conditions. However, general recommendations include:
• Powder Form:
Start with 1 teaspoon (5 grams) once or twice daily. Gradually increase the dose based on tolerance, but do not exceed 2 teaspoons (10 grams) per day to avoid digestive upset.
• Capsules:
If using capsules, follow the manufacturer's instructions, typically ranging from 1 to 2 capsules taken 1-2 times daily. It is advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement, especially for those with existing health conditions or those taking other medications.
