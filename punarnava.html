Interactive Learning of the punarnava

Botanical Name:

Boerhaavia diffusa

Common Names:

Punarnava (meaning "that which rejuvenates")
spreading hogweed
red spiderling
tarvine.

Uses in Ayush:


Punarnava is renowned for its diverse therapeutic applications, which include:
Diuretic:
Promotes urination, helping to relieve fluid retention.
Anti-inflammatory:
Reduces inflammation in various conditions.
Hepatoprotective:
Protects the liver from damage.
Antioxidant:
Fights oxidative stress, potentially preventing chronic diseases.
Antimicrobial:
Exhibits activity against various pathogens.
Anti-diabetic:
Helps in managing blood sugar levels.
Rejuvenator:
Used as a "Rasayan" in Ayurveda for its anti-aging properties and to boost immunity.

Directions to Use:


For quick relief, Punarnava can be prepared in various forms:
Decoction:
Boil 1-2 teaspoons of dried Punarnava root or leaves in water for 10-15 minutes.
Strain and drink the liquid.
This can help with conditions like urinary issues and inflammation.
Powder:
Take 1-2 grams of Punarnava powder mixed with honey or warm water.
This is effective for digestive issues and as a general tonic.
Juice:
Fresh leaves can be crushed to extract juice.
Consuming 10-20 ml of this juice can provide quick relief for digestive problems and enhance liver function

Dosage:

General Dosage:
The typical dosage of Punarnava varies based on the form used:
Powder:
1-2 grams, taken once or twice daily.
Decoction:
50-100 ml, taken once or twice daily.
Juice:
10-20 ml, taken once daily.
