Interactive Learning of the Alovera

Botanical Name:

Aloe barbadensis miller.

Common Names:

Some of the local names for aloe vera include:
• Aloe (English)
• Grithikumari (Hindi)
• Gwar Patha (Hindi)
• Ghrit Kumari (Sanskrit)
Description:
Aloe vera is a stemless or very short-stemmed succulent plant growing to 60–100 cm (24–39 in) tall. The leaves are lanceolate, thick and fleshy, green to grey-green, with white flecks on the upper and lower leaf surfaces. The leaf margin is serrated and has small white teeth. The flowers occur in summer on a spike up to 90 cm (35 in) tall, each flower pendulous, with a yellow tubular corolla 2–3 cm (0.8–1.2 in) long.

Uses in Ayush:

Aloe vera has many medicinal properties and uses:
• The leaves are widely used to heal burns, wounds and other skin ailments.
• It is used for inflammatory bowel conditions such as gastritis, irritable bowel syndrome, ulcerative colitis.
• It is used in menstrual cramps, irregularity and suppressed menses in females.
• It is a common ingredient in modern cosmetics.
• It is used as a deworming agent, for skin diseases, dysentery, minor wounds and burns.

Directions to Use:


For instant relief, the clear gel inside the aloe vera leaves can be directly applied to burns, wounds, and skin irritations. The gel is odorless, tasteless, and free of leaf skin or yellow parts.

Dosage:

There is no standard dosage for aloe vera, as it is not approved by the FDA for any medical condition. Oral ingestion of aloe vera extracts can be dangerous, as it causes reactions which are not yet fully understood. It is especially dangerous for pregnant women. Some people have allergic reactions, even when the aloe vera is applied only to the skin.
