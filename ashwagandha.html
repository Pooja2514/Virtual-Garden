Interactive Learning of the Ashwagandha

Botanical Name:

Withania somnifera

Common Names:

Ashwagandha, Indian Ginseng, Winter Cherry, Clustered Winter Cherry, Strychnos, Poison Gooseberry, Asgandha, Peyette

Uses in Ayush:

It helps the body resist stress and promoting overall wellness. Its traditional uses include:
Stress and Anxiety Relief:
Ashwagandha is known to reduce stress and anxiety levels, functioning similarly to certain prescription medications.
• Cognitive Function:
It may enhance memory and brain function.
• Immune Support:
The herb boosts the immune system and has antioxidant properties.
• Reproductive Health:
It supports sexual health and fertility.
• Pain Relief:
Ashwagandha has anti-inflammatory properties that may alleviate pain.
• Sleep Aid:
It is often used to improve sleep quality.

Directions to Use:

For quick relief, Ashwagandha can be taken in various forms:
• Powder:
Mix 1 teaspoon (approximately 3-5 grams) of Ashwagandha root powder with warm milk or water. This can be consumed once or twice daily.
• Capsules:
Standardized capsules containing Ashwagandha extract can be taken according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually ranging from 300-600 mg per dose.
• Tea:
Boil 1 teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder in water for 10 minutes, strain, and drink. This can be consumed once or twice daily.

Dosage:

The recommended dosage of Ashwagandha varies based on the form used:
• Powder:
3-5 grams per day.
• Capsules:
300-600 mg of standardized extract, taken once or twice daily.
• Tincture:
2-4 mL of a 1:2 tincture, taken two to three times a day. It's advisable to start with a lower dose and gradually increase it while monitoring for any side effects.
