Interactive Learning of the Guduchi

Botanical Name:

Tinospora cordifolia

Common Names:

Guduchi (Sanskrit), Amrita (Sanskrit)
• Giloy (Hindi)
• Tinospora (English)

Uses in Ayush:

Guduchi is renowned for its diverse therapeutic applications, including:
• Immunomodulation:
Enhances immune response.
• Anti-diabetic:
Helps in managing blood sugar levels.
• Antipyretic:
Reduces fever.
• Anti-inflammatory:
Alleviates inflammation.
• Antioxidant:
Protects against oxidative stress.
• Anti-arthritic:
Useful in treating arthritis.
• Hepatoprotective:
Protects the liver.
• Antimicrobial:
Effective against various pathogens.
• Digestive Aid:
Improves digestion and alleviates dyspepsia.
• Skin Disorders:
Treats skin diseases and promotes healing of wounds.

Directions to Use:


1. Guduchi Decoction:
• Boil 1-2 teaspoons of dried Guduchi stem pieces in 2 cups of water.
• Reduce to half the volume and strain.
• Drink this decoction once or twice daily for benefits such as fever reduction and immune support.
2. Guduchi Powder:
• Grind dried Guduchi stems into a fine powder.
• Mix 1 teaspoon of this powder with warm water or honey and consume once daily for digestive issues and general health.
3. Guduchi Juice:
• Fresh Guduchi stem can be juiced.
• Consume 30-50 ml of Guduchi juice daily for detoxification and to boost immunity.
Instant Relief Applications:
• For Fever: Drink Guduchi decoction or juice to help lower fever and enhance recovery.
• For Digestive Issues: Use Guduchi powder mixed with warm water to alleviate symptoms of dyspepsia and improve digestion.

Dosage:

The recommended dosage of Guduchi based on the form used:
• Decoction:
1-2 cups per day.
• Powder:
1 teaspoon (3-5 grams) once or twice daily.
• Juice:
30-50 ml per day.
